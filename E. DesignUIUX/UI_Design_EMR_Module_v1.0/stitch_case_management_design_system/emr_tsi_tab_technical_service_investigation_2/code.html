Premature Lubrication Failure

Primary Cause

Investigation reveals localized thermal breakdown of the ISO VG 68 synthetic lubricant. Operating temperatures exceeded the critical threshold (95°C) for sustained periods, leading to rapid oxidation and a significant drop in operational viscosity. This loss of fluid film integrity resulted in metal-to-metal contact within the bearing assembly.

psychology Contributing Factors

  • Ambient facility temperature +5°C above seasonal norm.
  • Inadequate ventilation cross-flow around the motor housing.
  • Slight angular misalignment detected during laser coupling check.
